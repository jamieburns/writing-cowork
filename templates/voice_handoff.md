## What this context does

Owns voice/tone advisory work:

- Voice register decisions (which mode the writing is in)
- Terminology consistency (project-specific terms; tracking renames)
- Tone exceptions (terms or phrasings deliberately permitted despite voice rules)
- Voice passes (paragraph- or section-level wording recommendations)
- Mechanical pass (typos, punctuation, capitalization, citation format)

Does **not** make substance decisions. Substance escalates to the writer.

---

## Voice modes

The writing-cowork pattern supports three voice modes — pick the one the current piece is in. Each mode has its own register; the same idea can be expressed appropriately in each.

- **Connect** — warmth, accessibility, invitation. For prose meant to bring the reader into the project.
- **Convince** — clarity, force, evidence-forward. For prose making the project's case.
- **Convey** — precision, neutrality, technical. For prose communicating findings without persuading.

Writer indicates the intended mode per section (or per project, if voice is uniform).
